The Alhambra is a 13th–14th-century Moorish palace-fortress in [[Granada]], set on the Sabika hill overlooking the city against the Sierra Nevada. It is Spain's second most-visited attraction (after [[La Sagrada Família]]) and one of the finest examples of Islamic architecture in Europe. UNESCO World Heritage Site (with the Albayzín).

## How to Book

- Online: **www.alhambra-patronato.es** — book 2–3 months ahead for peak season
- Entry to Palacios Nazaries is limited to **300 people every 30 minutes**; ticket specifies an exact time slot — do not be late
- A few leftover tickets are sometimes available at the ticket office on the day, but this is rare in peak season
- Audio guide: €6 (recommended)
- Getting there: Bus C30 from Plaza Isabel la Católica

## What to See

### Alcazaba
The original 13th-century citadel; oldest part of the Alhambra.
- **Torre de la Vela** (Watchtower): the most important tower; best viewpoint over Granada's rooftops and the Albayzín

### Palacios Nazaries (Nasrid Palaces)
The stunning centrepiece; the highpoint of Islamic art in Europe.

**Mexuar**: Entry hall and council chamber; 14th-century; later converted to a Christian chapel. Geometrically carved wooden ceilings.

**Palacio de Comares**: The most formal reception area.
- *Patio de los Arrayanes* (Courtyard of the Myrtles): calm rectangular pool flanked by myrtle hedges; seven harmonious arches
- *Salón de los Embajadores* (Hall of Ambassadors): the Alhambra's largest room; domed marquetry ceiling of 8,000 cedar pieces representing the seven heavens of Islam

**Palacio de los Leones**: Built 2nd half of 14th century under Mohammed V.
- *Patio de los Leones*: 12 marble lions support a fountain; 124 slender columns; the courtyard layout symbolizes the Islamic vision of paradise
- *Sala de dos Hermanas* (Hall of the Two Sisters): dizzying *muqarnas* (honeycomb-vaulted) dome of 5,000 cells
- *Sala de los Abencerrajes*: legendary octagonal stalactite ceiling; the site of alleged murders of the noble Abencerraj family

**Jardines del Partal**: Terraced gardens added in the early 20th century; a reflecting pool in front of the Palacio del Partal.

### Generalife
The sultans' gorgeous summer estate; soothing gardens, fountains, water channels.
- *Patio de la Acequia*: long rectangular garden pool; the heart of the Generalife
- *Patio de la Sultana*: a 700-year-old cypress tree shades the courtyard
- *Escalera del Agua*: water channels running down a shaded staircase

### Palacio de Carlos V
A Renaissance palace (begun 1527), incongruously inserted into the Alhambra by Holy Roman Emperor Charles V.
- Houses **Museo de Bellas Artes** (free for EU citizens) and **Museo de la Alhambra** (free)

## History Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| ~900 CE | First reference to *al-qala'a al-hamra* (the Red Castle) |
| 1237 | Mohammed I, Nasrid dynasty founder, moves court to Granada |
| 1302–09 | Generalife built as the sultans' summer palace |
| 1333–54 | Yusuf I begins Palacios Nazaries |
| 1350–60 | Palacio de Comares built |
| 1362–91 | Mohammed V builds Patio de los Leones |
| 1492 | Reconquista: Boabdil surrenders to the Reyes Católicos |
| 1527 | Palacio de Carlos V begun by Christian rulers |
| 1829 | Washington Irving "rediscovers" the neglected Alhambra; writes *Tales of the Alhambra* |
| 1984 | UNESCO World Heritage designation |

## Practical Tips

- **Book as early as possible** — this cannot be overstated; Alhambra tickets are the hardest in Spain to get
- Once inside Palacios Nazaries, you cannot re-enter after leaving — plan your route carefully
- Do not be late for your Palacios Nazaries time slot
- Strollers are not permitted in Palacios Nazaries or Generalife; leave them at the services pavilion
- The **Parador de Granada** (inside the grounds, a former convent) is a fabulous place for a meal even if not staying
- Allow 3–4 hours minimum; half a day or more for a thorough visit
- Combine with Albayzín's **Mirador San Nicolás** for sunset — a perfect day in Granada
