Barcelona is Catalonia's vibrant capital and Spain's most visited city, renowned for Antoni Gaudí's iconic architecture, the Gothic Quarter, Mediterranean beaches, and a world-class food scene. Population approximately 1.6 million.

## Top Sights

### [[La Sagrada Família]]
Gaudí's unfinished masterpiece basilica; the most-visited sight in Spain.
- Adult €20; guided tour or audio guide extra
- Hours: 9am–8pm (Apr–Sep), 9am–7pm (Oct–Mar)
- **Book online in advance** — sells out weeks ahead
- Planned completion ~2026

### Park Güell
Gaudí's hillside park with iconic mosaic terraces and dragon staircase.
- Ticketed zone: adult €10; book online
- Hours: 8am–9:30pm (summer), shorter in winter
- Free areas accessible anytime; arrive early to avoid crowds

### Casa Batlló
Gaudí's "House of Bones" on Passeig de Gràcia.
- Admission from €29; book online

### Gothic Quarter (Barri Gòtic)
Medieval labyrinth of narrow streets; Barcelona's oldest district.
- Free to explore; Barcelona Cathedral free (donations welcome)
- Best in the morning before tour groups arrive

### El Born / La Ribera
Trendy neighbourhood adjacent to the Gothic Quarter.
- Basílica de Santa Maria del Mar: free Gothic church
- Museu Picasso: adult €12; free first Sunday of month and every Thursday after 5pm
- Palau de la Música Catalana: UNESCO-listed concert hall; guided tours from €22

### Montjuïc
Hill southwest of centre with gardens, museums, fortress, and Olympic sites.
- Fundació Joan Miró: adult €14
- MNAC (Museu Nacional d'Art de Catalunya): adult €12; free first Sunday of month
- Castell de Montjuïc: adult €5
- La Font Màgica: free light and music show (Fri–Sat evenings, seasonal)

### Barceloneta & Beaches
Barcelona's main beach district. Best May–Jun or Sep–Oct; packed Jul–Aug.

## Getting There & Around

- **Fly**: Barcelona El Prat Airport (BCN); Aerobus to Plaça Catalunya €6.75 one way (~35 min)
- **By AVE from Madrid**: ~2.5 hours
- **City transport**: T-Casual 10-trip card €11.35; covers metro, bus, tram (Zone 1)

## Practical Tips

- **Best time**: May–Jun or Sep–Oct (warm, manageable crowds)
- Book all Gaudí sights online: Sagrada Família weeks ahead; Park Güell days ahead
- Passeig de Gràcia walk: Casa Batlló, Casa Milà (La Pedrera), and the "Block of Discord" in one route
- Pickpockets are common on La Rambla and in the Gothic Quarter — keep bags in front
- La Boqueria market is very touristy; Mercat de Santa Caterina is a better alternative

## Food Highlights

- *Pa amb tomàquet* (bread rubbed with tomato): staple Catalan dish
- Pintxos bars in El Born; seafood restaurants in Barceloneta
