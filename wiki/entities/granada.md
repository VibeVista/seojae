Granada is a city in the foothills of the Sierra Nevada in Andalucía. It was the last Moorish stronghold in Spain (surrendered 1492), and that legacy infuses every corner — from the [[Alhambra]] palace complex to the Albayzín quarter's whitewashed *cármenes* and the street-level tradition of free tapas with drinks. Population approximately 227,631; elevation 680m.

## Top Sights

### [[Alhambra]]
Spain's most iconic monument; a Moorish palace-fortress of breathtaking artistry.
- Adult €14; book 2–3 months in advance — tickets sell out
- See [[Alhambra]] for full details

### Capilla Real (Royal Chapel)
Last resting place of the Reyes Católicos (Queen Isabel I and King Fernando II).
- Adult/child €5/free; hours: 10:15am–6:30pm Mon–Sat, 11am–6pm Sun
- Fernando's sword, Isabel's silver crown and sceptre on display
- The monarchs' actual coffins lie in the crypt beneath the ornate marble tombs

### Catedral de Granada
Renaissance cathedral built atop the former main mosque.
- Adult/child €5/free; hours: 10am–6:30pm Mon–Sat, 3–5:45pm Sun

### Albayzín
Granada's historic Moorish quarter; UNESCO World Heritage site.
- Free to explore; expect to get lost (part of the charm)
- **Mirador San Nicolás**: classic sunset viewpoint over the Alhambra — arrive before sunset
- Bus C31 (circular): runs 7am–1am (to 10pm on Fri–Sat); every 8–15 min from Plaza Nueva
- *Cármenes*: private walled houses with terraced fruit and flower gardens; some open as museums

### Sacromonte
Granada's historic Roma barrio; flamenco cave-houses (*cuevas*) and live performances.
- **Museo Cuevas del Sacromonte**: €5; 10am–8pm (mid-Mar–mid-Oct), 10am–6pm (mid-Oct–mid-Mar)
- Best experienced on a guided walk; many local tour operators

### Hammam Al Ándalus
Granada's finest Moorish-style baths.
- From €38; with massage from €50; hours: 10am–midnight
- Reservations required; the dim, tiled rooms are suitably sybilline and relaxing

### Basílica San Juan de Dios
One of Andalucía's most opulent baroque interiors — stunning gold and silver altarpiece.
- Adult/child €5/free; 10am–8pm Mon–Sat (Sep–May), 2–8pm Sun Jun–Sep

## Granada Card

Adult €36–40, child €11; covers 10 city monuments including the Alhambra + free city bus trips.
- Book at www.granadatur.com or 858 88 09 90

## Practical Tips

- **Alhambra tickets**: Book at www.alhambra-patronato.es; 2–3 months ahead in peak season. Bus C30 from Plaza Isabel la Católica.
- **Walk in Granada**: Free (pay-what-you-like) tours from Plaza Nueva at 11am daily; highly recommended for orientation
- Entry to Palacios Nazaries is limited to 300 people every 30 minutes — stick to your time slot
- Free tapas with every drink ordered — a Granada tradition nearly extinct elsewhere in Spain
- Night visits to Alhambra (Tue & Sat Apr–mid-Oct; Fri & Sat mid-Oct–Mar): magical; covers Palacios Nazaries only; €8

## Getting There & Around

- **By AVE from Madrid**: ~3.5 hours (some routes require change at Antequera-Santa Ana)
- **By bus from Seville**: ~3 hours (ALSA buses, several per day)
- **City buses**: C30 → Alhambra; C31 → Albayzín (circular)

## Events

- **Semana Santa (Mar–Apr)**: Silent Thursday candlelit march; Wednesday *fraternity* parade to Sacromonte
- **Feria del Corpus Christi (May–Jun)**: Granada's big annual party; ~60 days after Easter; dancing, flamenco, parades
- **Festival Internacional de Música y Danza (Jun–Jul)**: World-class concerts at the Alhambra

## Food Highlights

- **Free tapas with drinks**: Granada maintains the tradition — each drink comes with a free tapa
- *Remojón granadino*: orange and salted cod salad
- Granada Tapas Tours: small-group food tours from €40
