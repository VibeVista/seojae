Seville (Sevilla) is the capital of Andalucía and Spain's fourth-largest city, set on the Río Guadalquivir. It blends Moorish palaces, a towering Gothic cathedral, passionate flamenco culture, and some of Spain's best tapas. Population approximately 688,710.

## Top Sights

### Catedral de Sevilla & La Giralda
World's largest Gothic cathedral (completed 1506) and its iconic minaret-turned-bell-tower.
- Adult/child €16/free; rooftop guided tours €16; audio guide €3
- Free entry Monday 4:30–6pm (limited numbers; reserve slot on website)
- Hours: 11am–3:30pm Mon, to 5pm Tue–Sat, 2:30–6pm Sun (Sep–Jun); extended hours Jul–Aug
- **La Giralda**: 104m tower climbed via 35 ramps (no stairs); the defining symbol of Seville
- Inside: Tomb of Christopher Columbus; major artworks by Zurbarán, Murillo, Goya
- **Do not visit Alcázar and cathedral on the same day** — too much to take in

### Real Alcázar
Magnificent Mudéjar palace complex; one of Europe's finest examples of Moorish-influenced architecture.
- Adult/child €11.50/3/free; free Monday 4:30–6pm (Oct–Mar only)
- Hours: 9:30am–7pm (Apr–Sep), 9:30am–5pm (Oct–Mar)
- **Pre-purchase tickets online** — entry queues are extremely long
- Highlights: Patio de las Doncellas, Salón de los Embajadores, Patio del Crucero, terraced gardens
- Featured as a filming location for *Game of Thrones* (Water Gardens of Dorne)

### Plaza de España & Parque de María Luisa
Seville's grandest civic space and its elegant central park.
- **Both free**
- Plaza de España: built for 1929 Ibero-American Exposition; tiled alcoves for each Spanish province; boat hire €6 for 35 minutes
- Parque de María Luisa: 34-hectare park; open 8am–10pm (midnight Jul–Aug)

### Barrio de Santa Cruz
The old Jewish quarter; a maze of whitewashed alleys, flower-filled patios, and charming plazas.
- Free to explore; most atmospheric in the evening
- Adjacent to the Alcázar gardens

### Hospital de los Venerables Sacerdotes
17th-century hospice turned museum; one of Seville's most rewarding art stops.
- Adult/child €10/8/free; hours: 10am–8pm (Mar–Jun), 10am–6pm (Dec–Feb)
- Works by Velázquez, Zurbarán, and Murillo; ornate chapel; Patio

### Museo de Bellas Artes
One of Spain's premier art museums; 17th-century paintings focused on Seville's Golden Age.
- EU/non-EU citizens free/€1.50; hours: 9am–9pm Tue–Sat, 9am–3pm Sun

### Triana
Colorful barrio across the Guadalquivir; home of Seville's ceramics industry and flamenco roots.
- Centro Cerámica Triana museum: adult/child €2.10/free; 11am–5:30pm Tue–Sat
- Riverside bars and restaurants; more local atmosphere than Santa Cruz

### Basílica de La Macarena
Home to Seville's most revered religious treasure, the Virgen de la Esperanza Macarena.
- Basilica: free; museum: €5
- Hours: 9am–2pm & 5–9:30pm Mon–Sat, 9:30am–2pm & 5–9pm Sun

## Events

### Semana Santa (Holy Week)
- **When**: Palm Sunday to Easter Sunday (Mar–Apr)
- Elaborate *pasos* (floats carrying religious statues) paraded through the city nightly
- Best views near the cathedral in the early evening
- **Book accommodation a year in advance** — the city fills completely

### Feria de Abril (April Fair)
- **When**: Two weeks after Easter (Apr)
- Six days of flamenco dancing, horse parades, music, and food on the Real de la Feria fairground
- Mostly local; visitors welcome to observe

### Bienal de Flamenco
- **When**: September (even-numbered years)
- Major international flamenco festival with performances, exhibitions, and workshops

## Getting There & Around

- **By plane**: Sevilla Airport (SVQ), 10km from centre; bus EA to city centre ~30 min (€4)
- **By AVE from Madrid**: ~2.5 hours (~€60–90)
- **City transport**: tram, bus, and Seville's bikeshare (SEVICI)

## Practical Tips

- **Best time**: Mar–Apr (Semana Santa + Feria), May–Jun, Sep–Oct. Jul–Aug can exceed 40°C.
- Book Alcázar and cathedral tickets online in advance — always
- **Free walking tours**: Pancho Tours (tip-based; excellent orientation)
- **Flamenco shows**: Tablao Los Gallos, Casa de la Memoria — book ahead
- **Aire Baños Árabes**: Moorish-style hammam (€37 including drink); a highlight after a long day

## Food Highlights

- **Best tapas**: Bar-Restaurante Eslava (near La Macarena) — local favourite, not touristy
- *Pavías* (beer-battered cod); *espinacas con garbanzos* (spinach with chickpeas)
- Azotea rooftop bar (El Centro): views + food
- Day trips to Jerez de la Frontera (~1 hour) for sherry bodega tastings
