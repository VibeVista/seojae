Toledo is a UNESCO World Heritage walled city 70km south of Madrid, often called the "City of Three Cultures" for its historic coexistence of Christian, Muslim, and Jewish communities. The entire old city sits on a rocky promontory encircled by the Río Tajo. Population approximately 84,000.

## Top Sights

### Catedral de Toledo
One of Spain's greatest Gothic cathedrals; construction 1226–1493.
- Adult €12.50; free Sunday morning
- Highlights: El Transparente (baroque altarpiece), paintings by El Greco, Goya, Rubens; the Sacristía; the Tesoro with a 500kg gold monstrance

### Sinagoga del Tránsito & Museo Sefardí
14th-century synagogue with stunning Mudéjar stucco decoration; museum of Sephardic Jewish culture.
- Adult €3, reduced €1.50; free Saturday afternoon and Sunday morning

### Museo del Greco
Museum in a historic house near the old Jewish quarter; extensive El Greco collection.
- Adult €3/free; free Saturday afternoon and Sunday morning
- Key work: *Vista y plano de Toledo*; 12 Apostle portraits

### Iglesia de Santo Tomé
Houses El Greco's greatest masterpiece, *El entierro del Conde de Orgaz*.
- Adult €3/free
- Short visit but unmissable for El Greco fans

### Alcázar de Toledo
Hilltop fortress with sweeping views; now the Army Museum.
- Adult €5/free; free Sunday
- Panoramic views over the city

## El Greco in Toledo

El Greco (Domenikos Theotokopoulos, 1541–1614) lived in Toledo from 1577 until his death. His elongated, spiritual style became synonymous with the city.

Key sites:
- **Iglesia de Santo Tomé**: *El entierro del Conde de Orgaz* (essential)
- **Museo del Greco**: largest collection
- **Catedral**: several works in the Sacristía

## Practical Tips

- **Pulsera Turística** (Tourist Bracelet): €10; covers Catedral, Sinagoga del Tránsito, Museo del Greco, Iglesia de Santo Tomé, and more — excellent value
- Toledo is best as a **day trip from Madrid** (30 min AVE from Atocha)
- Start early — streets are steep and crowds build by midday
- The old town is entirely pedestrian; wear comfortable shoes
- Best viewpoint: Mirador del Valle (across the Río Tajo, requires taxi or car)

## Getting There

- **AVE from Madrid Atocha**: 30 minutes, ~€13–15 each way
- **Bus from Madrid Estación Sur**: ~1 hour, cheaper but slower
- Toledo train station is a 20-minute walk from the old town (or take a taxi)

## Specialty Food

- **Marzipan (mazapán)**: Toledo's signature sweet; sold in every bakery. The Santo Tomé brand is one of the oldest.
- **Carcamusas**: local pork and vegetable stew
- Roast game (partridge, rabbit) is typical in Castilla-La Mancha
