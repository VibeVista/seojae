Segovia is a UNESCO World Heritage city in Castilla y León, 90km northwest of Madrid, renowned for its remarkably intact 1st–2nd-century Roman aqueduct, a fairy-tale Alcázar castle, and a magnificent Gothic cathedral. Population approximately 52,000.

## Top Sights

### Acueducto de Segovia (Roman Aqueduct)
One of the best-preserved Roman aqueducts in the world.
- **Free** (exterior only — the aqueduct itself is the attraction)
- 728m long, up to 28m high; 166 arches; built without mortar in the 1st–2nd century CE
- Best viewpoint: Plaza del Azoguejo at the lower end, where the aqueduct dramatically spans the square

### Alcázar de Segovia
Fairy-tale castle on a rocky promontory; said to have inspired Disney's Cinderella Castle.
- Adult €5.50, child €3/free; Torre de Juan II: extra €2.50 (worth it for views); audio guide €2.50
- Hours: 10am–7pm (Apr–Oct), 10am–6pm (Nov–Mar)
- Inside: lavishly decorated royal chambers, armory, panoramic views over the plateau

### Catedral de Segovia
Spain's last Gothic cathedral, completed 1768.
- Adult €3/free; free Sunday morning 10am–1pm
- Nicknamed "La Dama de las Catedrales" (Lady of the Cathedrals)
- Peaceful interior; museum with tapestries and an El Greco painting

## Getting There

- **Avant train from Madrid Chamartín**: 30 minutes, ~€10–12 each way
- **Bus from Madrid Moncloa**: ~1.5 hours, ~€5 each way
- Segovia train station is 2.5km from old town; take bus 11 to the aqueduct

## Practical Tips

- The aqueduct, cathedral, and Alcázar form a natural walking route through the old town
- Can combine with Toledo for consecutive day trips from Madrid
- The aqueduct is equally photogenic by night — schedule a late bus back if possible
- **Avoid summer midday**: Castilian plateau heat can be extreme (35°C+)

## Specialty Food

- **Cochinillo asado** (roast suckling pig): Segovia's most famous dish; the pig is roasted at 2–3 weeks old and so tender the meat is traditionally "carved" with a plate. Famous restaurants: Mesón de Cándido (by the aqueduct), El Bernardino.
- **Ponche segoviano**: local pastry filled with marzipan-like cream
